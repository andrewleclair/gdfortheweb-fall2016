At night in the summer, Joyce leaves the door open and sits on the steps.

She is the mysterious artist in residence.

Last summer, you and friend were passing by after a meal at Julian's.

A man was taking out the trash, rearranging the bins. Your friend asked if he would show you inside.

You were startled but the tour was amazing.

He was the carpenter, and has been working on the house for thirty years. He has a dog that follows him around; you've seen them before at the gas station.

They had just restored the frescoes upstairs.

Downstairs, the paneling of the double parlor is dark, dark maple and brilliant stained glass. The ceilings are endless; there are four floors.

The detailing was like nothing else you've ever seen.

Two cats occupy the circular room on the first floor. It is the most opulent litter box situation you have ever seen.

In the adjacent lot, they are growing sunflowers.

You pass two little boys on the corner, waiting for the early bus to pick them up. Their hair is parted perfectly.

At the corner, you cross over towards Seven Stars.
