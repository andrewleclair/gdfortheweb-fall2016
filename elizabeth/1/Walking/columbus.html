The gold dome caps the structure. Mosaic tiles spell out "Uptown Theater" at the entrance. Inside the gilded glass doors, a sandwich board leans against stacked packages of water bottles, with notices of upcoming gigs. The red and gold of the interior seems quieter in the clear of the morning.

To the left of the main entrance, a barber shop. The blue, red, and white pole turning at a timeless speed.

A small wood sign, illegibly weathered, suggests former inhabitants of the second floor.

Once, we went inside, through all of the rooms upstairs, the cursive lettering still visible on the doors, black letters edged in gold. "Dance Studio." A private tour of a formerly public place.
