The house on the corner opposite is huge; the windows extra tall. Red geraniums hang cheerfully from the second floor; the sprinkler is operated on a precise schedule. The whole corner feels cared for; established in a way that seems it has always been so.

The other day, in passing, the upstairs neighbor mentions that the house was renovated and restored about three years ago. Before that, it was abandoned.

The light changes, and you cross.
